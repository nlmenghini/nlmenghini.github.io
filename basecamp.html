    • 1. So who are you?

    I’m a designer; Product Designer by paid profession, designer by passion. I get excited by all facets of design; digital and physical, especially typography. I like things clean, clear, and orderly. My coworkers call my pursuit of pixel perfection “Nick-picking.” My professors called my designs “engineered.” That love of design and desire for simple grid based layouts intersect for me in software.

    2. What do you do currently?

    I work at UrbanBound; a Relocation Management Software provider. We help companies move employees and we do it through a tailored experience across 5 web applications. I create and maintain the visual identity and experience of those applications. I’ve been designing and growing at UrbanBound for 7 years now and I very much enjoy it.

    3. Why leave a good job after 7 years?

    I'm having a wonderful run at UrbanBound. I’ve learned quite a lot in the 7 years working here. Each year I experience personal and professional growth, tackle new challenges, and take on responsibility. The time has come to test out what I’ve learned in a new space. I’m ready for that next step and it feels right to take it when things are at a high point. Basecamp is that next step for me.

    4. Why hire someone willing to leave a good job?

    Why wait until the job is no longer desirable? I’m choosing to leave because the opportunity to work for one of the best doesn’t come around often. My skill set that has come as a result of hard work with creative people is exactly what I want to bring to Basecamp. I want to start the new chapter in my career and test my mettle.

    5. So why this job?

    Moving past the fact that jobs at Basecamp are highly sought after, my driving interest is working on HEY. I joined UrbanBound because they aim to solve the unpleasant experience of moving. I know that pain having moved a fair amount in my life and it resonated with me. I want to work on products that reinvent and refresh old ways of thinking. The chance to work on HEY and other Basecamp products offers that exact opportunity.

    6. OK, so what are we getting?

    A Swiss army knife. Your application says you hire a product designer that can do it all. For years I’ve been doing the bulk of UX, UI, and a fair bit of front end development at UrbanBound . Throw in a good amount of QA and design for other areas of the business and you would get a designer that can do a great many things if you need him to.


    You get somebody who wants the opportunity to make change. Somebody who wants to work with a great cast of people. Somebody who wants to create and build clean, compelling, intuitive designs. I saw this posting and it excited all the parts of me that enjoy my current working environment and then some. Basecamp is the opportunity to preserve everything I currently love about work and gain so much more.

    Standardized Reporting

    The project aimed at completely rebuilding our employer reporting system. The current system made it hard to make copies of reports, save filter sets, and schedule exports.

    We already knew the core set of reports that we would offer. I did some initial design exploration to help narrow down the possible columns and filters for each report. The card based reports page provides an easy, scannable interface with a limit set of reports. I designed the reports around a notion of versioning to kill the clutter of endless copies. An employer user can save any number of data sets and filters as a version. Once saved an employer can schedule any number of exports for that version.

    Relocation Plan

    A relocation is long and complicated process. At the center is a policy that drives what an employee can do, how we can help them, and how much an employer wants to spend.

    The challenge with the plan was that each user has different needs from the same set of information. The employee app I focused on managing the budget and reviewing/booking services. The consultant app I focused on the ability to provide guidance. The employer app I focused on highlighting the spend of the move.

    Order and Lead Management System

    The Order and Leads Management System (OLMS) is a refresh of UrbanBound's existing supplier application. The focus was to create a new order process that would allow suppliers to create many cost estimates for a single order.

    Cost Calculator

    Calculating costs is a huge part of any relocation. UrbanBound puts a high priority on being transparent with these costs. An in app cost calculator is something we have talked about for some time. This was part a team project for a hackathon.

    While my teammates figured out the proper math and back end coding, I prototyped the calculator. My goal was to keep with the spirit of transparency. The more information you provide the more accurate your estimate becomes. Even though the form narrows down the total you never lose sight of all potential costs.

    A cost breakdown form for relocations that becomes more accurate as you provide more information.
